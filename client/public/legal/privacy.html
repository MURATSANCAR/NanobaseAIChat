Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how NanobaseAI (“NanobaseAI”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you use our AI collaboration platform, including chat, agents, file features, and the NanobaseAI Operation Center (the “Service”).

This Policy applies to users of deployments operated by us or on behalf of your organization. Where your employer or another organization provisions your account, that organization may act as an independent controller for certain processing activities. In those cases, their policies may also apply.

1. Data controller and contact

For questions about this Policy or to exercise your privacy rights, contact us at privacy@nanobase.ai or via https://www.nanobase.ai.

2. Information we collect

Depending on how the Service is configured, we may process:

  • Account data: name, email address, username, authentication identifiers, role, and organization membership.
  • Conversation and usage data: prompts, messages, attachments, metadata (timestamps, session identifiers), and feature usage necessary to operate the Service.
  • Operation Center data: job identifiers, execution records, audit events, tool outcomes, and generated artifacts (for example reports or exports) when you use operation workflows.
  • Technical data: IP address, device and browser type, logs, and security signals.
  • Support and communications: information you send when you contact support or respond to surveys.

We do not intentionally collect special categories of personal data (such as health or biometric data) unless you choose to include them in prompts or files. Please avoid submitting sensitive personal data unless your organization has approved it.

3. How we use information

We use personal information to:

  • provide, maintain, and secure the Service;
  • authenticate users and enforce access controls configured by your administrator;
  • route requests to AI models and operational tools enabled for your workspace;
  • generate, store, and deliver outputs you request (including Operation Center artifacts);
  • monitor performance, troubleshoot errors, and prevent abuse or fraud;
  • comply with law and respond to lawful requests;
  • improve the Service where permitted by contract and applicable law.

4. AI processing

The Service may send prompts and related context to AI models and automation components configured by your organization. Outputs are generated probabilistically and may be inaccurate or incomplete. Your organization controls which models and connectors are enabled.

Where Operation Mode is enabled, prompts may be processed through a dedicated operation orchestration layer rather than general-purpose chat models. Operational metadata (such as job status and audit events) is retained to support traceability and compliance needs defined by your administrator.

5. Legal bases (EEA, UK, and Switzerland)

Where GDPR or similar laws apply, we rely on:

  • Contract: to deliver the Service under our agreement with you or your organization.
  • Legitimate interests: security, fraud prevention, and service improvement, balanced against your rights.
  • Consent: where required for optional cookies or features.
  • Legal obligation: where we must retain or disclose data by law.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

We use essential cookies and similar technologies for authentication, session management, and security. Optional analytics or preference cookies, if used, will be described in your deployment’s cookie notice or administrator settings.

7. Sharing and subprocessors

We may share information with:

  • cloud infrastructure and hosting providers;
  • AI and API providers enabled in your workspace configuration;
  • identity, email, or payment providers where applicable;
  • professional advisers and authorities when required by law;
  • a successor entity in connection with a merger or asset sale, subject to safeguards.

We require subprocessors that handle personal data to implement appropriate contractual and security measures. A current subprocessor list may be provided on request to enterprise customers.

8. International transfers

If personal data is transferred outside your country, we use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms, unless an adequacy decision applies.

9. Retention

We retain personal information for as long as needed to provide the Service, fulfill legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Retention periods for conversations, logs, and Operation Center records may be set by your organization’s administrator.

10. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect personal information, including access controls, encryption in transit, and monitoring. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

11. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, or port your personal data, and to object to or withdraw consent for certain processing. You may also lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. To exercise rights, contact privacy@nanobase.ai. If your account is organization-managed, we may direct you to your administrator.

12. California and other U.S. state privacy rights

Where U.S. state privacy laws apply, residents may have additional rights regarding access, deletion, correction, and opt-out of certain sharing for targeted advertising. We do not sell personal information as defined by the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended. Authorized agent requests will be verified as required by law.

13. Children

The Service is not directed to children under 16 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

14. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. We will post the revised version with an updated “Last updated” date and, where appropriate, provide additional notice through the Service or to administrators.
